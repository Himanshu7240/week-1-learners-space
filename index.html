All about Cricket History

Cricket, a sport with roots tracing back to 16th century England, evolved from a rural pastime to an international phenomenon. The first recorded match was played in 1646, and by the 18th century, it had gained popularity across England. The Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC), founded in 1787, established the first formal rules. Cricket's global expansion began with British colonization, leading to the formation of the first international match in 1844 between the USA and Canada. The sport's pinnacle, Test cricket, started in 1877 between England and Australia. Today, cricket is celebrated worldwide, particularly in countries like India, Australia, England, and the West Indies, with formats ranging from Tests to One-Day Internationals (ODIs) and Twenty20 (T20) matches.

Basic rules

Cricket is played by two teams of 11, with one side taking a turn to bat a ball and score runs, while the other team will bowl and field the ball to restrict the opposition from scoring. The main objective in cricket is to score as many runs as possible against the opponent

Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli, a legend in Indian cricket, is a right-handed batsman admired for his exceptional skills and aggressive style. He holds numerous records, including the fastest to 70 international centuries, and has captained the Indian national team across all formats. Currently playing for Royal Challengers Bangalore in the IPL, Kohli remains a dominant force in the cricketing world.
